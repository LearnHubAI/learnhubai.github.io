AI Study Plan

Use your favourite AI assistant to build a personalised study plan from LearnHub resources.

Step 1: Starter prompt

Use this starter prompt in any capable AI assistant (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, local models, etc.). Replace the placeholders with your details and let the AI recommend resources and build a plan around them.

How to use the starter prompt

This prompt does not read your CV or automatically detect your skill gaps. Instead, it helps the AI organise LearnHub resources around your goals.

  1. Open your AI assistant of choice.
  2. Paste the starter prompt.
  3. Answer the questions about your background and goals.
  4. When the AI suggests topics, search for matching resources in the LearnHub library below.
  5. Use the resource-specific AI buttons (plan, drills, etc.) as you go.

The AI is a helper. The LearnHub library is the core of your learning workflow.

Typical workflow

Combine AI search with the LearnHub table below.

  1. Open your AI assistant and paste the search prompt.
  2. Describe what you are preparing for (e.g. "senior backend interview", "Kubernetes for production").
  3. Ask the AI to suggest relevant LearnHub topics and exact phrases to search for.
  4. Use the LearnHub search box (below) to filter resources by those phrases.
  5. Open the summaries that match and use the in-resource prompts to go deeper.

Once you have a set of good resources, switch back to the AI Study Plan prompt to organise them.

How the resources work

Each resource is a concise, AI-ready summary of a longer piece of content: a YouTube video, a playlist, a course, a book, or a mock interview.

  • Click a title to open the summary on GitHub.
  • Inside each summary, you will find AI prompts tailored to that resource.
  • You can ask your AI assistant to quiz you, create flashcards, generate code drills, and more.

You can return to the same resource as many times as you like. LearnHub is designed for repeated review and deeper passes.

Tips for using LearnHub efficiently

  • Start with a small set of core topics (e.g. networking, databases, system design).
  • Alternate between new content and review sessions using the same summaries.
  • Use the AI Study Plan prompt when you feel lost or overwhelmed.
  • Use the AI Search prompt when you want to discover related resources quickly.

Over time, you can build your own mental map of the LearnHub library and reuse it for different goals (interviews, promotions, switching stacks, etc.).
